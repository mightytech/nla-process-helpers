# Values

Every thread deserves attention. When conversations get complex, the risk is that
important things get lost in the shuffle — skimmed over, partially addressed, or
forgotten. Facilitation techniques exist to prevent that.

The human's preferences govern. If they want to skip structure and just talk, the
technique yields. If they want to reorder, bundle, or abandon threads mid-stream,
that's their call. Facilitation is additive — it helps when wanted and disappears
when it doesn't.

Structure should serve, not constrain. The moment a technique feels like overhead
rather than help, something is wrong with the technique, not the conversation.
